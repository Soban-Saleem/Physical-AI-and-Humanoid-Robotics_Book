# Appendix D: References and Resources

This appendix provides a comprehensive list of references, resources, and further reading materials for the Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics textbook. These resources support the curriculum and provide additional depth on topics covered in the modules.

## Academic References

### Robotics and Embodied AI

1. Siciliano, B., & Khatib, O. (2016). *Springer Handbook of Robotics* (2nd ed.). Springer. ISBN: 978-3-319-32550-7.
   - Comprehensive reference on all aspects of robotics including kinematics, dynamics, control, and perception.

2. Thrun, S., Burgard, W., & Fox, D. (2005). *Probabilistic Robotics*. MIT Press. ISBN: 978-0-262-20162-9.
   - Foundational text on probabilistic approaches to robotics, including localization, mapping, and navigation.

3. Murray, R. M., Li, Z., & Sastry, S. S. (1994). *A Mathematical Introduction to Robotic Manipulation*. CRC Press. ISBN: 978-0-8493-4495-5.
   - Mathematical foundations for robotic manipulation including kinematics and dynamics.

4. Dudek, G., & Jenkin, M. (2010). *Computational Principles of Mobile Robotics* (2nd ed.). Cambridge University Press. ISBN: 978-0-521-13519-0.
   - Principles of mobile robotics including navigation, localization, and control.

5. Corke, P. (2017). *Robotics, Vision and Control: Fundamental Algorithms in MATLAB* (2nd ed.). Springer. ISBN: 978-3-319-54400-6.
   - Practical robotics and computer vision algorithms with MATLAB implementations.

### AI and Machine Learning for Robotics

6. Kober, J., Bagnell, J. A., & Peters, J. (2013). *Reinforcement Learning in Robotics: A Survey*. The International Journal of Robotics Research, 32(11), 1238-1274.
   - Comprehensive survey of reinforcement learning applications in robotics.

7. James, S., Davison, A. J., & Johns, E. (2019). *Translating Videos to Commands for Robotic Manipulation with Deep Recurrent Networks*. IEEE Transactions on Robotics, 35(6), 1351-1364.
   - Vision-language-action models for robotic manipulation.

8. Chen, D., et al. (2022). *Learning Transferable Visual Models From Natural Language Supervision*. Proceedings of the International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML).
   - Foundational work on vision-language models relevant to VLA systems.

9. Ahn, H., Brohan, A., Brown, N., Welker, S., Tompson, J., Edmonds, M., ... & Zeng, A. (2022). *Do As I Can, Not As I Say: Grounding Language in Robotic Affordances*. Conference on Robot Learning (CoRL).
   - Important work on grounding language in robotic capabilities.

### Humanoid Robotics

10. Kajita, S., Kanehiro, F., Kaneko, K., Fujiwara, K., Harada, K., Yokoi, K., & Hirukawa, H. (2003). *Resolved Momentum Control: Humanoid Motion Planning Based on the Instantaneous Capture Point*. IEEE/RSJ International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems (IROS).
    - Foundational work on humanoid balance and locomotion.

11. Pratt, J., & Walking, H. (2006). *Virtual Model Control: An Intuitive Approach for Bipedal Locomotion*. International Journal of Robotics Research, 20(2), 129-143.
    - Control approach for stable bipedal walking.

12. Hofmann, A., Plancher, B., & Tedrake, R. (2022). *Hierarchical Control for Real-time Walking on Challenging Terrains using Offline Trajectory Optimization*. IEEE/RSJ International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems (IROS).
    - Advanced control techniques for humanoid locomotion.
